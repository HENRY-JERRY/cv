Technology acceptance in Uganda

There are many obstacles countries face in integrating ICT in their curricula. These may range from lack of proper ICT infrastructure, inappropriateness of teachers knowledge and skills, constraints in resources to sustain the program and stakeholders’ unwillingness to implement and use ICT. This research work aimed at addressing teachers’ and students’ attitudes towards teaching and learning of subsidiary ICT a new subject for high schools decreed by the government of Uganda in 2012 under its MoES. The purpose of this project was to devise a technique and model of boosting technology awareness and acceptance among stakeholders in Uganda secondary schools by using the measure of their willingness, and elucidate their intentions in relation to their attitudes. Other aspects that were considered are subjective norms, perceived usefulness, and perceived ease of use, computer self-efficacy, and technological complexity since they are very important as suggested by (Davis, Bagozzi, & Warshaw, 1989) in Technology Acceptance Model (TAM) approach.
